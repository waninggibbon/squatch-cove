# Scouts of Ghost Story Cove - GM Bible

## Campaign Overview

**Tone:** Gravity Falls meets Stranger Things (leaning comedic)

A troop of teenage scouts becomes stranded on a mysterious PNW island after a night of ghost stories. The next morning, the Scoutmaster is missing, the boat is gone, and the stories are beginning to come to life.

---

# Premise (Player Facing)

The scouts wake to discover:

- Scoutmaster missing
- Boat missing
- Cabin door open

The troop must uncover the truth and escape the island.

---

# The Truth (GM Only)

Thirty years ago, a scout named Tim was lost on the island and became the Hermit.

The Hermit discovered the island's secret:

> Stories told around the ancient campfire become real.

Lonely after decades of isolation, he:

- Stole the boat
- Kidnapped the Scoutmaster
- Tried to create a permanent troop

However, the story magic spiraled out of control and created **The Listener**, a manifestation formed from all the scouts' ghost stories.

The Listener kidnapped the Scoutmaster from the Hermit and appears capable of breaking the normal rules of island magic.

---

# Island Story Magic

## Rules

1. Stories become real.
2. Manifestations fade after a few days.
3. Manifestations cannot normally leave the island.
4. Manifestations behave according to their stories.

## The Problem

The Listener appears to be breaking Rules 3 and 4.

---

# Adventure Structure

## Act 1: Cabin Investigation

Clues:

- Urine-written note from Scoutmaster
- Giant footprints
- Scoutmaster's map

### Note

"Dear scouts,

I went out to get some berries. I should be back before morning but if I'm not, I'll be due north of here.

P.S. Sorry for the smell. Couldn't find a pencil."

---

## Act 2: Bigfoot

Tutorial combat.

Bigfoot is:

- Innocent
- Confused
- Going through an existential crisis

Key revelation:

- He did not take the Scoutmaster.
- There is another person on the island.

---

## Act 3: Airstream Trailer

Mirror puzzle:

"The key is all that you can see.
Use what you can find to go through me.
But beware of what you use.
For with life it will be imbued."

Any item may become the key.
The chosen item comes to life and must be defeated.

---

## Act 4: Cryptid Encounter

Use one of the players' campfire stories.

The cryptid guards an important clue.

---

## Act 5: The Hermit

Fight the Hermit.

Reveal:

- He stole the boat.
- He kidnapped the Scoutmaster.
- He wanted a troop and a friend.
- The Listener took the Scoutmaster from him.

The Hermit becomes an uneasy ally.

---

## Act 6: The Listener

Final confrontation at the ancient campfire.

Victory requires:

- Combat
- Investigation
- Understanding the story magic
- Helping the Hermit let go

The players ultimately defeat the Listener by creating a better story.

# Rules Reference

## Making Checks

Roll:

d20 + relevant stat

### Difficulties

| DC  | Difficulty |
| --- | ---------- |
| 10  | Easy       |
| 15  | Moderate   |
| 20  | Hard       |
| 25  | Heroic     |

---

## Stats

### Strength

- Melee attacks
- Breaking objects
- Lifting
- Grappling

### Dexterity

- Dodging
- Sneaking
- Climbing
- Acrobatics

### Constitution

- Health
- Endurance
- Resisting harm

### Intelligence

- Investigation
- Reasoning
- Knowledge
- Ranged weapon damage

### Wisdom

- Perception
- Tracking
- Tool use
- Reading people

### Luck

Luck score equals rerolls per session.

After rolling:

"I spend Luck."

Reroll the die.

Take the new result.

---

## Combat

### Initiative

Everyone rolls:

d20 + Dexterity

Highest goes first.

---

### Attacking

Roll:

d20 + relevant stat

Melee attacks generally use Strength.

Ranged attacks generally use Intelligence.

If the attack equals or exceeds Defense:

Hit.

---

### Damage

Examples:

| Weapon             | Damage |
| ------------------ | ------ |
| Pocket Knife       | d4     |
| Club / Flashlight  | d6     |
| Axe / Heavy Weapon | d8     |
| Monster Attack     | varies |

Add Strength to melee damage.

Add Intelligence to ranged damage.

---

### Defense

Defense = 10 + Dexterity

---

### Hit Points

HP = 10 + (Constitution × 2)

---

## Equipment Tags

When an item's tag clearly applies:

+2 to the roll.

Examples:

### Rope

- Bind
- Reach
- Climb

### Camcorder

- Record
- Observe

### Skateboard

- Speed
- Impact

### Signal Mirror

- Reflect
- Signal

Use common sense.

If the item would help, it probably grants +2.

---

## Conditions

### Restrained

Cannot move.

### Afraid

Disadvantage on next roll.

### Inspired

Advantage on next roll.

---

## Camp Clout (CC)

Maximum: 3

Earn CC when players:

- Make the table laugh
- Lean into flaws
- Roleplay well
- Come up with creative solutions

Spend CC to:

- Reroll a check
- Reduce incoming damage by 5
- Introduce a useful minor item
- Create a helpful coincidence

---

## Merit Badges

Each scout may use their Merit Badge ability once per session.

These automatically succeed if the action is clearly related to the badge.
