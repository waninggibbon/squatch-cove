# Scouts of Lost Pine Island

## Campaign Overview

**Tone:** Gravity Falls meets Stranger Things

- Primarily comedic and adventurous
- Occasional spooky moments
- Mystery-focused
- Emotional but not overly serious
- Family-friendly

Players take on the role of teenage scouts stranded on a mysterious island in the Pacific Northwest after a camping trip goes horribly wrong.

---

# Premise (Player Facing)

A troop of scouts travels to a remote island in the Pacific Northwest for a weekend camping trip.

On their first night, they gather around the campfire and tell ghost stories.

One story about Bigfoot is particularly frightening.

The next morning, the scouts discover:

- Their Scoutmaster is missing.
- The boat is gone.
- The cabin door is standing open.

With no way off the island, the scouts must work together to uncover the truth behind the disappearance and find a way home.

---

# The Truth (GM Only)

Thirty years ago, a scout named Tim disappeared on the island during a camping trip.

Everyone assumed he died.

In reality, Tim survived.

Over decades, he became known as **The Hermit**.

While stranded on the island, Tim discovered its secret:

> Stories told around the ancient campfire become real.

The stronger the story and the more people believe it, the stronger the manifestation.

The Hermit spent decades learning how the island's magic worked.

When the current troop arrived, the Hermit became obsessed with recreating the childhood he lost.

Using the island's magic to his advantage, he:

- Stole the boat.
- Kidnapped the Scoutmaster.
- Attempted to create a permanent troop for himself.

However, the magic spiraled out of control.

Instead of creating a normal cryptid or monster, the stories combined to create something new:

**The Listener.**

The Listener broke the normal rules of the island.

It kidnapped the Scoutmaster from the Hermit.

Now both the scouts and the Hermit must stop it before it escapes the island and becomes permanent.

---

# Island Story Magic

The Hermit understands several important rules.

### Rule 1

Stories become real.

### Rule 2

Manifestations usually disappear after a few days.

### Rule 3

Manifestations normally cannot leave the island.

### Rule 4

Manifestations behave according to the story that created them.

Examples:

- Bigfoot is shy because the stories portrayed him that way.
- Living objects act according to their nature.
- Cryptids generally follow the expectations of the story.

### The Problem

The Listener appears to be breaking these rules.

The Hermit believes it may be capable of leaving the island without losing its power.

# Adventure Structure

## Act 1: The Missing Scoutmaster

### Cabin Investigation

The scouts wake up and discover:

- Scoutmaster missing
- Boat missing
- Cabin door open

### Clues

#### Scoutmaster's Note

A scrap of paper smelling strongly of urine:

> Dear scouts,
>
> I went out to get some berries. I should be back before morning but if I'm not, I'll be due north of here.
>
> P.S. Sorry for the smell. Couldn't find a pencil.

#### Giant Footprints

Large footprints surround the area.

Evidence appears to point toward Bigfoot.

#### Scoutmaster's Map

A map with several locations marked.

One location appears particularly important.

---

## Act 2: Bigfoot

The scouts track the footprints.

Eventually they encounter Bigfoot.

### Tutorial Combat

Bigfoot initially appears hostile.

Combat begins.

The fight should teach the game's mechanics.

After a few rounds, it becomes clear that:

- Bigfoot is not trying to kill anyone.
- Bigfoot is frightened.
- Bigfoot is confused.

### Bigfoot Reveal

Bigfoot eventually communicates.

Examples:

> Bigfoot surrender!

> Hairless scouts violent!

> Bigfoot not take Scoutmaster!

Bigfoot reveals:

- He did not kidnap the Scoutmaster.
- There is another person on the island.
- Strange things have been happening.

### Bigfoot's Secret

Bigfoot remembers the campfire.

He remembers nothing before that.

He is beginning to suspect that he is not a naturally occurring creature.

This gives him an ongoing existential crisis throughout the adventure.

---

## Act 3: The Airstream Trailer

The scouts discover an abandoned trailer.

Inside are clues pointing toward the island's supernatural nature.

### Mirror Puzzle

The trailer door locks.

Steam or condensation reveals a message:

> The key is all that you can see.
>
> Use what you can find to go through me.
>
> But beware of what you use.
>
> For with life it will be imbued.

The players may use any object as a key.

Whatever object they choose becomes animated.

### Living Key Encounter

Examples:

- Flashlight → living spotlight creature
- Rope → animated knot monster
- Camcorder → floating documentary monster
- Compass → navigator creature

After defeating the manifestation, the scouts gain:

- Additional clues about the Hermit
- Information about the island's story magic
- A lead toward the Hermit's camp

---

## Act 4: The Cryptid Encounter

The scouts encounter a dangerous manifestation created from one of the campfire stories.

Ideally, this creature comes directly from a player's own story.

This should be the first truly dangerous combat encounter.

The cryptid guards an important clue or item.

Examples:

- A map
- A key
- Scoutmaster's belongings
- Evidence connecting the Hermit to the disappearance

---

## Act 5: The Hermit

The scouts locate the Hermit's camp.

### The Camp

The camp is filled with decades of scavenged scouting supplies.

Examples:

- Homemade merit badges
- Old troop banners
- Empty uniforms
- Scout records
- Reserved seating around a campfire

One seat has clearly been prepared for the Scoutmaster.

### Hermit Combat

The Hermit initially attempts to capture the scouts.

He fights using:

- Rope traps
- Slingshots
- Smoke bombs
- Scoutcraft gadgets

The fight should feel more like fighting an overgrown scout than a villain.

### The Reveal

The Hermit eventually reveals the truth.

He admits:

- He stole the boat.
- He kidnapped the Scoutmaster.
- He wanted a troop again.
- He wanted a friend.

However:

> The Listener took the Scoutmaster from him.

The Hermit is heartbroken and terrified.

He becomes an uneasy ally.

---

## Act 6: The Listener

The Listener has taken the Scoutmaster.

Its ultimate goal is unclear.

The Hermit believes it seeks permanence.

### Final Encounter

The confrontation occurs near the island's ancient campfire.

The encounter is a combination of:

- Combat
- Investigation
- Problem solving
- Roleplaying

Defeating the Listener cannot be accomplished through combat alone.

The players must understand and engage with the island's story magic.

### Possible Resolution

The scouts tell a new story.

A better story.

One where:

- The Scoutmaster is rescued.
- The Hermit finds closure.
- Bigfoot finds belonging.
- The Listener loses its power.

The island's magic responds.

Reality changes.

The adventure concludes.

# Running Themes

## Growing Up

The Hermit represents someone who never moved on from childhood.

## Stories Have Power

Stories shape reality throughout the adventure.

## Fear Creates Monsters

Many threats exist because people believe they should.

## Friendship and Belonging

Both Bigfoot and the Hermit are searching for connection.

## Choosing Your Own Story

The players ultimately determine how the story ends.

# Camp Clout (CC)

Camp Clout is the game's shared narrative currency.

Players earn CC when they:

- Make the table laugh.
- Roleplay their character well.
- Lean into their flaws.
- Come up with creative solutions.
- Create memorable moments.

Players may spend CC to:

- Reroll a check.
- Reduce incoming damage.
- Introduce a useful minor item.
- Create a helpful coincidence.

Maximum CC held at one time: 3.
